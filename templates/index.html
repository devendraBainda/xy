About This Technology

Understanding our AI-powered lung cancer classification system

Deep Learning Model

Our system uses a fine-tuned ResNet50 model trained on thousands of lung tissue samples to accurately classify different types of lung tissues.

High Accuracy

The model achieves over 97% accuracy in distinguishing between benign tissue, squamous cell carcinoma, and adenocarcinoma.

Privacy First

All image processing happens on our secure server. We don't store your images permanently or share them with third parties.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our lung cancer classification system

What types of lung cancer can this system detect?

Our system can classify lung tissue images into three categories: Lung Benign Tissue (non-cancerous), Lung Squamous Cell Carcinoma, and Lung Adenocarcinoma (both cancerous types).

How accurate is the classification?

Our model achieves over 97% accuracy on validation data. However, it should be used as a supportive tool for medical professionals, not as a replacement for clinical diagnosis.

What image formats are supported?

The system supports JPG, JPEG, and PNG image formats. For best results, use high-quality, well-lit images of lung tissue samples.

Is this tool intended for clinical use?

No, this tool is for educational and research purposes only. It should not be used as a substitute for professional medical diagnosis. Always consult with healthcare professionals for medical advice.
